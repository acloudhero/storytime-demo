The Ferryman's Lantern

The ferryman kept one lantern trimmed and one in reserve, because the
river did not care which night a wick would fail. When the first light
guttered out mid-crossing, he did not curse the dark; he simply reached
for the second, lit it, and rowed on.

A failed light, he liked to say, is not a lost crossing. It is only a
crossing that waits for a steady hand to begin again.
